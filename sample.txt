Keegan Murray sets rookie record for 3-pointers made</h1><p class="ArticleHeader_ahSubtitle__4qa5C">The No. 4 overall pick, Murray records his 188th 3-pointer to best the single-season rookie mark set by Donovan Mitchell in 2017-18.</p></div><div class="ArticleHeader_ahBy__2ddjx"><div class="ArticleHeader_ahAuthor__NbBw0"><div class="ArticleAuthor_base__LYqxf"><div src="https://www.nba.com/watch/embed/1076654?autostart=false&#038;mute=true" allowfullscreen></iframe></div><p class="wp-caption-text">Keegan Murray&#039;s clean 3-point stroke has him etched in the rookie record book.</p></div>
<p>With Paolo Banchero having put <a href="https://www.nba.com/news/kia-rookie-ladder-march-29-2023-edition">the Kia Rookie Ladder</a> in a virtual headlock from nearly opening night, there has been limited time in the spotlight for what&#8217;s developed into a strong rookie class.</p>
<p>But 2022 <a href="https://www.nba.com/draft/2022">No. 4 overall pick</a> Keegan Murray of the Sacramento Kings wrested momentary control on Wednesday, sinking his 188th 3-pointer of the season (462 3-point attempts, 40.7% on 3-pointers) to seal the single-season rookie record for most makes from long distance. Donovan Mitchell, then playing for the Utah Jazz, set the record at 187 in 2017-18.</p>
<p class="Component-root-0-2-271 p Component-p-0-2-261">Murray broke the record with with 6:44 to go in the third quarter to set the record, flashing an understated grin after the shot fell as he finished with 13 points in the game in Portland.</p>

<p>Fittingly, the record came in a record-breaking night for the Kings at large as they topped the Trail Blazers, <a href="https://www.nba.com/game/sac-vs-por-0022201145">120-80</a>, to clinch a playoff berth. The win guaranteed Sacramento of its first playoff berth since 2006 and the team’s 16-year playoff drought was the longest in NBA history. It was also the longest active postseason dry spell among teams in the NBA, NFL, NHL and Major League Baseball.</p>

<p>Murray becomes the third player to top 180 triples, and is the only player to stay over 40% on 3-pointers on more than 400 attempts. Saddiq Bey (&#8217;21-22: 175-460; 38%) and Rudy Fernandez (&#8217;08-09: 159-398; 39.9%) are the two closest on that front.</p>
<p class="Component-root-0-2-271 p Component-p-0-2-261">Murray’s milestone was not lost on Kings coach Mike Brown in the midst of the celebrating.</p>
<p class="Component-root-0-2-271 p Component-p-0-2-261">“Breaking any type of records at the highest level in anything you do is absolutely amazing. And to see Keegan do that tonight, with the way he’s played all year and how hard he’s worked, was an amazing feat,” Brown said. “You don’t take that for granted. That is hard to do when you’re talking about something in the history of something.”</p>
